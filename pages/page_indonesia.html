Indonesian Culture

Indonesia consists of over 17 000 islands in the Indian and Pacific oceans. It is the world's fourth largest country by population, and is also home to the largest Muslim population in the world. Indonesia's people come from over 1 000 ethnic groups, united by a shared identity with the motto of "Bhinneka Tunggal Ika", or "Unity in Diversity".

Cultural custom: Showing respect to elders

In Indonesian culture, it is considered very important to show respect for elders, which is conveyed through various rituals. One such ritual is putting one's forehead to an elder's hand as a form of greeting.

Cultural celebration: Independence day

On the 17th of August, Indonesians celebrate the proclamation of independence from Dutch colonial rule.

Cultural clothing: Batik

Batik is a type of shirt traditional to Indonesia. It is embroidered with a traditional pattern.

Cultural food: Cireng

Cireng originated from the western part of the island of Java. It consists of fried tapioca batter, and is mostly eaten as a snack. Tapioca is a type of starch extracted from the cassava tuber, which is common in many other Indonesian dishes. The cassava plant in native to Indonesia, and serves as a staple food for many people.
